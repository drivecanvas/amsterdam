You’re Committed

Car financing was invented almost 100 years ago and little has changed since then….why? Why do people need to commit to owning their car for years? Why feel compelled to stay in a single car for so long?

Link

It can be expensive

At times, owning a car can be costly. Once any warranty and/or maintenance packages expire, you’re on the hook for quite a bit: out-of-pocket upkeep, repairs, maintenance…the list goes on and it can be a lot! Not to mention, if you’re considering a used car, you’re really rolling the dice. Why can’t all of the be more like a set-it-and-forget-it experience?

Link

Buying is long-winded

On average, car shoppers spend more than a month researching an upcoming car purchase. And then on the day you’re ready to buy that car, you’ll spend 3+ hours with the seller! Why does the process need to be so lengthy?

Link

Selling is a pain

You can post your car in the classifieds, but it could take months to sell and involve countless back-and-forth conversations with strangers and people haggling with you. Why can’t the whole “getting rid of your car” process be as straightforward as giving back a rental car?
